Different Types of Online Casinos

Every online casino tries to stand out by being at least slightly different than its competitors, but they all tend fall within the same general categories:

Web-Based: These are the most common and are basically online casino websites with various casino games you can play from your browser. They’re easy to navigate and don’t require you to download anything.

Download-Based: Download casinos run as a program on your computer. You need to download and run them, similar to how you would play a computer game. Their advantage is often better graphics or gameplay, but these aren’t of much value to newer players.

Live-Based: Live dealer casinos run real-world games, which players can watch while online. For example, roulette players can place their bets online, and then watch the spin take place in real time on an actual roulette table. These can be a lot of fun but aren’t as readily available due to the extra cost to the casino.

Mobile-Based: Mobile casinos are apps that you can download and play on a smart phone or tablet. They generally offer the same experience as playing on a computer but on a smaller screen, and some companies offer you the option of using one account to play across multiple devices.

Choose the best online casino for you

  • Is It Secure? In order to play with real money, you’ll have to send some to the website, which most people do through a credit card. Players also need to submit their personal information, such as name, birthdate, address, etc. You may assume that you should be suspicious of online casinos that request this, but they have good reason. They will need this information to adhere to any security systems or gaming regulations, especially ones that are country-based. In fact, you should be concerned with any online casino that does not ask for this information. Your data will be safe as long as the site employs advanced encryption technology — at least 128-bit SSL (Secure Socket Layer). Online casinos usually have a page outlining their security measures, and many will mention it right on their home page.
  • Is It Regulated? Unfortunately, there is no single regulatory body to monitor all online gaming. Each country has its own set of rules and laws and carries the responsibility to enforce them. As a rule, every online casino requires a licence to operate. Most banks and financial institutions demand this before processing transactions, which is why it’s internationally respected. There are several different licensing bodies, though, and some are more reputable than others. Every online casino should clearly display information on their licence and the issuer, so you can easily look them up. If a site uses a reputable regulator, you can be sure that their practices and systems are reviewed and verified regularly.
  • Is It Fair? A huge concern for players — even at land-based sites — is whether the games are rigged. There seems to be an even greater fear with online casinos, as everything is run through computers. However, nearly every casino, whether in the real world or online, ensures fairness by using a ‘random number generator’ (RTN). These produce random numbers at a rapid speed — thousands of them per second. When a player hits ‘spin’ on a slot machine, or ‘deal’ on a virtual card game, whatever number the RTN is on at that time is translated into a slot outcome or card hand. It is instantaneous and absolute, so any action by a player (such as stopping the slot reels early or in a certain order) won’t affect the outcome. Casinos can control the odds of each machine, but the RTN ensures that the actual result is always random. Licensing bodies will also test the RTN of an online casino to make sure they are accurate. Some companies will also choose to opt-in to additional testing agencies, such as eCOGRA, who will run independent audits on every aspect of their site. If everything is legit, the site will be ‘certified’; legitimate sites are proud of this and will clearly display this on their homepage.
